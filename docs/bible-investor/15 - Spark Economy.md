## 1. Business Context

### 1.1 The Problem: Passive Streaming vs Active Engagement

**Spotify's Model (Passive)**:
- Fan streams song (doesn't require fan action)
- Artist gets $0.003
- Artist has no idea fan existed
- No feedback loop (artist can't respond)

**Problem**: No engagement. Fan is passive consumer, artist is content machine.

**NGN's Model (Active)**:
- Fan tips artist directly ("You're amazing! Here's $2")
- Artist gets $1.80 (90%)
- Artist sees fan's name, message (optional)
- Immediate feedback loop (artist responds, creates more content)

**Advantage**: Both fan and artist are engaged. Creates habit loop.

### 1.2 Precedent: Twitch Donations Prove It Works

**Twitch streamers** generate billions through:
- Direct tips from viewers ($1-500 per tip)
- 50/50 split with Twitch
- Streamers optimize for engagement (responding to donors, special shout-outs)
- Fans voluntarily pay because they feel valued

**Twitch economics**:
- $1B+ annual revenue from streamers ($500M+ to creators)
- 10 million active creators
- Average creator earning: $50K-500K/year (at scale)

**Why it works for music**: Same dynamic can work for artists.

## 2. The Spark Mechanic: How It Works

### 2.1 What Is a Spark?

**Spark** = Fan tip to artist (denominated in USD, displayed as "Sparks" in-app for engagement feedback)

**Where fans can Spark**:
- Artist's profile (post a $X Spark)
- Song/release (tip the song)
- Live stream (real-time tips during performance)
- Comment (tip to acknowledge a great comment)
- Social feed (tip any content)

**Spark amounts** (fan chooses):
- $0.50 Spark (small acknowledgment)
- $1.00 Spark (appreciation)
- $2.00 Spark (strong support)

## 8. Prevention of Spark Gaming

### 8.1 Fake Spark Detection

**System flags suspicious tipping patterns**:
- 100 tips in 1 minute from same account â†’ Flagged
- Tips always from new accounts â†’ Flagged
- Artist tips their own account â†’ Flagged
- Tips from known bot networks â†’ Flagged

**Action**:
- Suspicious tips excluded from artist balance
- Account reviewed/suspended if pattern continues
- Refunds issued to tips on fake Sparks

### 8.2 Why Sparks Are Hard to Game

**Unlike streams** (which can be faked with bots), Sparks require:
- Real credit card (can't fake payment)
- Real money movement (not just digital)
- Real user account (with identity verification for payout)

**Cost to game**: Hiring 100 people to tip $1 each = $100 spent to earn $90 (net negative)

**Result**: Gaming Sparks is economically irrational. System is self-policing.

## 12. Risks & Mitigations

### 12.1 Risk: What If Sparks Don't Drive Adoption?

**If artists don't tip each other (and fans don't tip artists)**:

**Mitigation**:
- Educational content (show artists how Sparks work)
- Incentive programs (match first $100 in Sparks)
- Social proof (highlight top earners)

**Probability**: Low. Twitch proves tipping model works.

### 12.2 Risk: What If Artists Feel Pressure to Beg for Tips?

**If Sparks become "required" for survival**:

**Mitigation**:
- Maintain other revenue streams (subscriptions, ticketing, B2B)
- Don't make Sparks mandatory
- Celebrate organic engagement (not forced)

**Probability**: Low. Twitch/Patreon don't have this problem.

### 12.3 Risk: What If Fans Get Fatigued by Tipping?

**If constant Spark requests become annoying**:

**Mitigation**:
- Set engagement guidelines (don't beg for tips)
- Limit notifications (don't spam fans)
- Focus on content (tips come naturally from great art)

**Probability**: Medium. Must manage carefully.
