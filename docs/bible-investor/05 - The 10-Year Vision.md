## 6. Risks to 10-Year Vision

### 6.1 Existential Risks (Could Derail Entire Plan)

#### Risk 1: Artist Sentiment Reverses
- **If**: Spotify raises per-stream payouts to $0.01+
- **Impact**: Independent artists less motivated to leave; NGN adoption slows
- **Mitigation**: Offer features Spotify can't (direct tips, transparent charts, rights verification)
- **Probability**: Low (Spotify profit-driven; unlikely to raise payouts)

#### Risk 2: Regulatory Backlash
- **If**: US/EU forbids or heavily taxes micropayments to creators
- **Impact**: Revenue model breaks; profitability impossible
- **Mitigation**: Diversify revenue (subscriptions, ticketing reduce micropayment dependence)
- **Probability**: Medium (possible but unlikely)

#### Risk 3: Spotify's Competitive Response
- **If**: Spotify launches competing artist platform in 2025
- **Impact**: Harder to acquire artists; network effects less pronounced
- **Mitigation**: Move fast (build 10K artist base before Spotify competes); emphasize transparency (NGN's advantage)
- **Probability**: High (but will take Spotify 2+ years to launch)

### 6.2 Execution Risks (Could Slow Growth)

- **Talent acquisition**: Hiring engineers, ops, partnerships in competitive market
- **Regulation complexity**: Music licensing in 30+ countries with different rules
- **Product execution**: Building competitor-quality product on limited budget
- **Partnerships**: Convincing radio stations, venues, labels to integrate

**Mitigation**: Strong board, experienced operations team, clear roadmap, aggressive hiring.

### 6.3 Market Risks (Could Limit Upside)

- **Market size**: If artist infrastructure TAM is smaller than $11.4B estimate
- **Adoption**: If artists don't actually adopt NGN despite dissatisfaction
- **Profitability**: If unit economics don't work at scale

**Mitigation**: Validate assumptions aggressively (2024-2025); adjust plan if needed.
