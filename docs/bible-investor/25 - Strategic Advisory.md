# Chapter 25: Strategic Advisory Ecosystem

## Executive Summary

NGN is building a **strategic advisor network** (not board, but close counsel) of music industry leaders, technologists, and creators who provide guidance on critical decisions. Advisory board includes: music attorney (rights issues), creator economy expert (retention), radio exec (partnerships), artist/creator (product feedback), technologist (scaling), and institutional partner (B2B strategy). Each advisor brings 15+ years expertise in their domain. Advisors are compensated with small equity stakes (0.05-0.1% each) and are called on ad-hoc for specific decisions. Result: Access to institutional wisdom at startup capital cost; credibility with investors, partners, and artists.

## 2. Advisory Board Operations

### 2.1 Meeting Schedule

**Quarterly all-hands meeting** (2 hours):
- All 6 advisors + CEO + COO
- Strategic topics (partnerships, retention, tech, artist feedback)
- Open discussion (advisors can challenge decisions)

**Monthly individual check-ins** (30-45 min):
- CEO + advisor (1:1)
- Focused on that advisor's domain
- Ad-hoc problems (radio partnership bottleneck? ask radio advisor)

**Async communication**:
- Email for quick questions
- Slack channel (all advisors + CEO)
- Quick feedback on proposals

### 2.2 Decision Process

**When CEO needs advice**:

```
1. Identify domain (is this legal? retention? tech?)
2. Message relevant advisor(s)
3. Provide context (problem statement, options)
4. Ask specific question (not vague "what should we do?")
5. Advisor provides written response + thinking
6. CEO decides (advisors inform, don't decide)
7. Update advisor (how decision played out)
```

**Example**:
- Problem: SMR data accuracy declining (radio stations submitting wrong artist names)
- Domain: Radio operations + Rights
- Ask radio advisor: "We're seeing 20% ambiguous artist matches. Is this normal? How do we improve?"
- Advisor responds: "Normal for early stage. Try these 3 techniques..."
- CEO implements one approach
- Follow-up: Tell advisor what worked

### 2.3 Advisor Responsibilities

**DO**:
✅ Share honest perspective (your experience in this domain)
✅ Challenge decisions (ask "why" if you disagree)
✅ Introduce contacts (leverage your network)
✅ Escalate risks (point out dangers you see)
✅ Celebrate wins (reinforce good decisions)

**DON'T**:
❌ Micromanage operations (that's CEO's job)
❌ Make decisions unilaterally (advisory role only)
❌ Compete with other advisors (unified team)
❌ Share confidential information (NDA required)
❌ Push own agenda (your company over NGN's success)

## 3. Advisor Selection Process

### 3.1 Criteria for Good Advisors

**Domain expertise**: 15+ years in their specific area (not generalists)

**Network**: Can open doors (relationships matter)

**Judgment**: Can see around corners (anticipate problems)

**Alignment**: Believe in NGN's mission (not chasing next startup)

**Availability**: Willing to give 3-5 hours/month (not too busy)

**Temperament**: Can disagree professionally (constructive, not defensive)

### 3.2 Finding Advisors

**Network recruiting**:
- Ask existing board members (who do they know?)
- Ask past mentors (who would help?)
- Ask happy customers (who do they respect?)

**Referral incentive**:
- Offer 0.05% equity for successful advisor referral
- Turn talented contacts into advisors

**Vetting**:
- Reference check (ask past companies)
- Coffee chat (assess fit + judgment)
- Legal review (ensure no conflicts)

## 4. Advisor Equity & Compensation

### 4.1 Equity Structure

**Each advisor gets**:
- 0.05-0.1% equity (vesting over 1 year)
- Option to participate in future rounds (pro-rata rights)
- Full cap table transparency

**Why equity?**:
- Aligns incentives (they benefit if NGN succeeds)
- Attracts quality people (meaningful upside)
- Avoids cash burden (early stage can't pay advisory fees)

**Example equity value**:
- Series A values NGN at $20M
- 0.1% equity = $20K value
- Grows with next round (if raises at $100M, worth $100K)

### 4.2 NDA & Advisory Agreement

**Every advisor signs**:
- NDA (confidentiality of strategic information)
- Advisory agreement (expectations, equity terms)
- 1-year vesting schedule (stay engaged to get equity)

**Standard terms**:
- Advisor role is advisory only (no formal decision authority)
- Time commitment: "Best efforts" (not guaranteed hours)
- Termination: Either party can exit with 30-day notice
- Equity: Vests over 1 year cliff

## 5. How Advisors Add Value

### 5.1 Value #1: Strategic Guidance

**Example**: Radio partnership strategy

Radio advisor says:
- "Don't approach SiriusXM first (too corporate, won't move fast)"
- "Start with independents (prove concept, faster integration)"
- "Then approach Townsquare Media (regional leader, will want to match competitor)"
- "SiriusXM will eventually reach out (FOMO)"

**Value**: Saves 12 months, avoids dead-ends, faster growth

### 5.2 Value #2: Relationship Introductions

**Example**: Label partnership

Label advisor introduces NGN to three label contacts:
- "You should talk to my friend Sarah at Sony (she's open to new tech)"
- "Also connect with Mike at Warner (he's more conservative, but worth trying)"
- "And Elena at Independent Label Group (early mover, will adopt)"

**Value**: Warm introductions beat cold calls 10:1. Saves months of outreach.

### 5.3 Value #3: Risk Spotting

**Example**: Expansion to podcasts

Creator economy advisor warns:
- "Podcast market is crowded (Spotify, Apple, Amazon all investing)"
- "Artist music creator ≠ podcast creator (different audiences)"
- "Better to own music first, then expand (not now)"

**Value**: Prevents misaligned spending, keeps focus on core business

### 5.4 Value #4: Credibility & Validation

**When fundraising**: "We have [Radio Exec] and [Music Attorney] on our advisory board"
- Investors trust these names (they've succeeded before)
- Signals NGN is serious (not just founders guessing)

**When pitching partners**: "Our advisor [Creator Expert] used this retention strategy at Patreon"
- Partners believe strategy (proven at scale)
- Reduces "unproven startup" skepticism

## 6. Advisory Board Success Stories

### 6.1 Example: Fixing SMR Data Quality

**Problem**: Radio stations sending bad artist names (20% ambiguous matches)

**CEO question**: "Is this normal? How do we scale with this problem?"

**Radio advisor response**:
- "This is normal in first 50 stations"

**Value**: Would've taken months to figure out alone; advisor shortened timeline by 3-4 months

## 9. Risks & Mitigation

### 9.1 Risk: Advisor Conflict of Interest

**If advisor has competing interest**:

**Mitigation**:
- Require disclosure upfront (avoid surprises)
- Recuse from decisions (don't vote on conflicts)
- Diversify advisors (no single person dominates)

**Probability**: Low (select carefully)

### 9.2 Risk: Advisor Disappears

**If key advisor becomes unavailable**:

**Mitigation**:
- Never depend on single advisor (diversified board)
- Document advice given (capture knowledge)
- Replace with new advisor (keep board fresh)

**Probability**: Medium (people get busy; normal)
