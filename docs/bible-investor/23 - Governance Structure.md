# Chapter 23: Institutional Governance Structure

## Executive Summary

NGN adopts **professional, role-based governance** (not founder-dependent) to signal institutional maturity to investors, partners, and artists. Board structure: (1) **Founder CEO**, (2) **Two independent directors** (investor + industry expert), (3) **Artist advisor** (representing community voice). Governance committees: Audit (financial oversight), Compensation (fairness), Rights (artist protection). Decision-making framework: CEO recommends, board approves (>$500K decisions). Result: Institutional credibility attracts enterprise partnerships (labels, radio networks) and institutional capital (Series A/B). Removes family references (eliminated "Rant Protocol", "Uncle Enforcer"), adopts professional terminology (director vs family titles). Artists trust professional governance (more likely to move earnings on platform).

## 1. Business Context

### 1.1 Why Governance Matters

**Investor view**:
- "Do they have a real board?" (indicates professionalism)
- "Is CEO replaceable?" (indicates sustainability)
- "Do they have financial controls?" (indicates integrity)

**Partner view**:
- "Can we trust them with our data?" (governance = trustworthiness)
- "Will they steal our revenue?" (board oversight = protection)

**Artist view**:
- "Is this a real business or a family operation?" (governance = legitimacy)
- "Will my earnings be safe?" (professional boards protect artist interests)

**Governance is not bureaucracy—it's credibility architecture.**

### 1.2 Current State vs Professional Model

**Current** (pre-Series A):
- Founder-led
- Informal decision-making
- No board oversight
- Family involvement

**Post-Series A** (professional):
- CEO-led with board oversight
- Formal decision-making processes
- Independent board review
- Family involvement fades (meritocratic)

## 2. Board Structure

### 2.1 Three-Person Board (Series A Stage)

#### Seat 1: Founder CEO

**Role**: Lead company, execute strategy, daily operations

**Qualifications**:
- Built previous products
- Music industry credibility
- Capital allocation experience

**Compensation**: Salary + equity (standard founder package)

**Decision authority**: Approve decisions <$500K, recommend decisions >$500K to board

#### Seat 2: Independent Investor Director

**Role**: Financial oversight, capital efficiency, institutional guidance

**Qualifications**:
- 10+ years venture investment
- Portfolio company board experience
- Music industry or Creator economy experience preferred

**Selection**: By Series A lead investor (negotiated right)

**Compensation**: Board fee ($30-50K annually) + small option package

**Responsibilities**:
- Audit committee chair
- Financial reporting oversight
- Budget approval
- Risk management

#### Seat 3: Independent Industry Director

**Role**: Artist protection, industry relationships, strategic partnerships

**Qualifications**:
- 15+ years music industry experience
- Label, radio, or artist management background
- Understands artist economics

**Selection**: CEO recommends, board approves

**Compensation**: Board fee ($30-50K annually) + option package

**Responsibilities**:
- Rights committee chair
- Artist issues escalation
- Radio station partnerships oversight
- Industry relationship management

### 2.2 Advisory Board (5-7 Members)

**Purpose**: Counsel without formal decision authority

**Members**:

**Advisor 1: Music Law Expert**
- Entertainment attorney
- Advises on rights, licensing, compliance
- 10+ years music industry legal

**Advisor 2: Financial Services**
- Former payment processor executive
- Advises on Stripe integration, payment compliance
- PCI/AML expertise

**Advisor 3: Creator Economy Expert**
- Founder of creator platform (Patreon, Substack, etc.)
- Advises on creator retention, monetization
- B2C platform scaling experience

**Advisor 4: Radio Industry Veteran**
- Former radio station GM or network executive
- Advises on radio partnerships, SMR strategy
- Industry relationships

**Advisor 5: Artist/Creator**
- Successful independent musician
- Represents artist community voice
- Provides product feedback

**Advisor 6: Technologist** (optional)
- CTO or engineering leader from unicorn company
- Advises on scaling infrastructure, hiring
- Technical credibility

**Advisor 7: Institutional Partner** (optional)
- Label executive or aggregator founder
- B2B partnership opportunities
- Institutional relationships

**Compensation**: 0.05-0.1% equity per advisor (no cash fees, incentive-aligned)

## 3. Governance Committees

### 3.1 Audit Committee

**Purpose**: Financial oversight, fraud prevention, compliance

**Members**: Investor director (chair), CEO, CFO (when hired)

**Meetings**: Quarterly

**Responsibilities**:
- Review financial statements (monthly)
- Approve expenses >$50K (pre-approval)
- Ensure proper accounting
- Tax/legal compliance
- Internal controls (fund safeguards)

**Key issue**: Artist fund safety
- Escrow accounts separated from operating funds
- Insurance coverage (fraud protection)
- Annual third-party audit

### 3.2 Rights Committee

**Purpose**: Artist protection, rights management oversight

**Members**: Industry director (chair), CEO, artist advisor

**Meetings**: Monthly (artist issues move fast)

**Responsibilities**:
- Artist dispute escalation
- Rights ledger integrity
- Payment accuracy oversight
- Artist communication strategy
- Community feedback

**Key issue**: Dispute resolution
- Artist vs artist conflicts (who owns what)
- NGN error recovery (if we misallocate funds)
- Vendor conflicts (Stripe disputes, chargebacks)

### 3.3 Compensation Committee

**Purpose**: Ensure fair pay, equity allocation

**Members**: Investor director (chair), Industry director, CEO

**Meetings**: Annually

**Responsibilities**:
- Salary benchmarking
- Equity grants
- Performance-based compensation
- Employee retention strategy

**Key issue**: Founder dilution
- Ensure founder retains meaningful equity after funding rounds
- Prevent excessive founder dilution (demoralizes team)
- Build talent retention (competitive salaries)

## 4. Decision-Making Framework

### 4.1 Authority Levels

**CEO Authority (No Board Approval)**:
- Decisions <$100K
- Hiring (within approved budget)
- Product roadmap (strategic direction already approved)
- Vendor contracts <$50K

**CEO + Board Consent (Approval Required)**:
- Decisions $100K-$500K
- New product lines
- Major partnerships (label deals, major radio networks)
- Organizational changes (restructure, new teams)

**Board Majority (Special Approval)**:
- Decisions >$500K
- New fundraising rounds
- Major M&A (sell or acquire)
- Policy changes (artist payout rates, fee structures)

**All Board (Unanimous)**:
- Removal of CEO or board member
- Liquidation or bankruptcy

### 4.2 Decision Documentation

**All board decisions documented**:
- Meeting minutes (what was discussed)
- Decision (what was approved)
- Rationale (why the board approved)
- Actions (who's responsible, deadline)

**Distributed to**:
- Board members
- CEO
- CFO
- General Counsel

**Purpose**: Audit trail (accountability + compliance)

## 6. Conflict of Interest Policy

### 6.1 What Is a Conflict?

**Example conflicts**:
- Board member owns competing company (direct competition)
- Board member has family in artist community (bias risk)
- CEO negotiates own salary (self-dealing)
- Director invests in vendor (financial incentive)

### 6.2 Conflict Handling

**Process**:
1. Disclose conflict to board
2. Recuse from discussion/voting on issue
3. Document disclosure (audit trail)
4. Decide if conflict is material (can business continue?)

**Example**:
- Industry director has brother who's NGN artist
- Brother's song gets copyright claim (ownership dispute)
- Director recuses from dispute resolution
- CEO + investor director decide outcome

## 7. Artist Protection Governance

### 7.1 Artist Advisory Board

**Separate from main board** (direct artist representation)

**Members**: 5-7 successful artists on NGN

**Quarterly meetings**:
- Artist pain points discussion
- Product feedback
- Fairness concerns
- Community needs

**Access to CEO + Board**:
- Can escalate issues directly
- Quarterly report to board (artist perspective)
- Direct input on artist-impacting decisions

**Compensation**: Quarterly Spark grants ($500-1,000 per artist)

### 7.2 Artist Rights Protection

**NGN commits**:
- ✅ No unilateral fee changes (90-day notice + artist vote)
- ✅ Transparent payout calculation (fully auditable)
- ✅ Artist data ownership (artists can export)
- ✅ Dispute escrow (money held safely during disputes)
- ✅ Insurance coverage (protect against NGN error)

**Board oversight**: Rights committee verifies quarterly

**Financial Compliance**:
- ✅ PCI DSS (payment processing security)
- ✅ SOC 2 Type II (operational controls)
- ✅ Tax withholding (1099 reporting to IRS)

**Data Compliance**:

**Music Licensing**:
- ✅ Mechanical licenses (for music composition)

**Board oversight**: Audit committee reviews quarterly

### 8.2 Insurance Coverage

**NGN carries**:
- D&O insurance (directors & officers liability, $5M+)
- Cyber liability (data breach protection)
- E&O insurance (professional liability)
- Fiduciary liability (artist fund protection)

**Cost**: ~$150K/year
**Benefit**: Protects against lawsuits, fraud, data breaches

## 9. Succession & Leadership Continuity

### 9.1 CEO Succession Planning

**Question**: "What if CEO gets hit by a bus?"

**Plan**:
- COO (Chief Operations Officer) designated as interim
- Board meets within 48 hours to decide:
  - Interim leadership (could be temporary COO)
  - Search for new CEO (internal or external)
  - Timeline (accelerated if interim is weak)

**By 2027**: Start hiring COO to build bench strength

### 9.2 Board Continuity

**Investor director**: Typically replaced by next investor (Series B)

**Industry director**: Can serve 3+ terms (build expertise)

**Founder CEO**: Can serve indefinitely (if performing well)

**Succession trigger**: Performance review annually
- Are they hitting metrics?
- Do they have board support?
- Is renewal in best interest of company?

## 12. Risk Mitigation

### 12.1 Risk: Board Member Conflicts

**If investor director has competing interest**:

**Mitigation**:
- Conflict disclosure required
- Recusal from voting
- Independent director decides
- Document all decisions

**Probability**: Low (board members incentivized to behave; reputation at stake)

### 12.2 Risk: Artist Backlash

**If artists feel unprotected**:

**Mitigation**:
- Artist advisory board provides voice
- Rights committee escalation
- Annual independent audit
- Public commitment to artist fairness

**Probability**: Low (NGN designed around artist protection)
