# Chapter 11: Revenue Streams & Unit Economics

## Executive Summary

NGN generates revenue from four diversified streams: **(1) Spark Tips** (5% platform fee on fan tips), **(2) Premium Subscriptions** ($9.99-24.99/mo for advanced features), **(3) Ticketing** (2.5% + $1.50/ticket on live events), **(4) B2B Services** (API licensing, data, enterprise). By 2027, NGN reaches profitability with $XXM annual revenue, 75%+ gross margins, and LTV/CAC ratios of 10-30x. Unit economics are validated: average artist lifetime value is $1,000-5,000; customer acquisition cost is $50-150 via radio/organic growth.

---

## 1. Business Context

### 1.1 Why Four Revenue Streams?

**Single-revenue-stream risk**: If NGN relied only on Spark tips (like Patreon), one policy change (Apple's cut of in-app tips) could destroy business.

**Diversification advantage**: Four streams reduce dependence on any single source:
- If Spark tips decline, subscriptions grow
- If subscriptions plateau, ticketing expands
- If ticketing matures, B2B licensing scales
- Network effects compound across all streams

**Historical precedent**:
- **Spotify**: Freemium subscriptions (80%) + ads (20%) = diversified
- **YouTube**: Ads (55%) + subscriptions (35%) + donations (10%) = diversified
- **Yelp**: Ads (97%) + other (3%) = concentrated (mistake)

NGN's four-stream model is **intentionally designed to avoid Yelp's over-concentration.**

### 1.2 Revenue Model Philosophy

**Core principle: Revenue should align with artist value creation.**

- **Spark tips**: Direct fan support (artist creates engaging content → fans tip)
- **Subscriptions**: Premium features that increase artist discovery/earnings
- **Ticketing**: Live event monetization (artists perform, fans attend)
- **B2B Services**: Enterprise value (data, API access, compliance tools)

**Anti-principle: We don't take cuts from artist earnings.**

Unlike Spotify (which takes 30% of all revenue), NGN's platform fee is explicit and capped:
- Spark tips: 5% fee only (95% to artist + 5% to NGN)
- Direct payouts: No fee (100% to artist)

This alignment ensures artists benefit when NGN succeeds.

## 2. Revenue Stream #1: Spark Tips (30-40% of Revenue)

### 2.1 What Are Sparks?

**Spark** = Fan tip to artist (denominated in USD, displayed as "Sparks" in-app)

- **Value**: $0.50 - $5.00 per Spark (fan chooses amount)
- **Usage**: Fans tip on artist posts, live streams, music releases
- **Direct**: 100% goes to artist balance instantly (net of 5% platform fee)

### 2.2 Unit Economics: Spark Tipping

#### Revenue Per Spark Transaction

| Spark Tier | Fan Amount | Platform Fee (5%) | Artist Nets | Processing Cost | NGN Margin |
|------------|-----------|------------------|-------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Small | $1.00 | $0.05 | $0.95 | $0.03 | $0.02 |
| Medium | $5.00 | $0.25 | $4.75 | $0.08 | $0.17 |
| Large | $10.00 | $0.50 | $9.50 | $0.15 | $0.35 |

**Key insight**: Processing costs are small; margin improves at scale.

#### Expected Spark Volume Per Artist

**Baseline** (inactive artist):
- 0-1 Spark/month
- Annual: $0-12

**Active** (engaged artist):
- 10-50 Sparks/month
- Annual: $120-600

**Viral** (breakout artist):
- 100-500 Sparks/month
- Annual: $1,200-6,000

**Megastar** (top 1%):
- 1,000+ Sparks/month
- Annual: $12,000+

**Platform average** (all artists): ~$300/artist/year (blended)

#### Expected Revenue: Spark Tips

**By 2027** (10,000 artists):
- Average Spark revenue per artist: $400/year
- Total artist earnings from tips: $4M
- NGN platform fee (5%): $200K annually
- Processing costs: ~$80K
- **NGN profit from Sparks: $120K/year**

**This seems small, but:**
- Spark tips are ~25% of artist earnings (not 100%)
- Other revenue streams add to this
- By 2030, with 50K artists, Spark revenue grows to $XXM+

### 2.3 Why Spark Tips Are Defensible

**Competitive advantage**: Sparks feel more valuable than abstract "supporting the artist" because:
- **Instant feedback**: Artist sees tip immediately on dashboard
- **Public recognition**: Fan name/comment appears on post (visible to others)
- **Novelty**: Feels like Twitch donations (which work for streamers)
- **Low friction**: One-click tipping (no subscription commitment)

**Barrier to entry**: Incumbents (Spotify, Apple) can't offer Sparks because:
- Spotify has 70% margin on subscriptions; offering 95% payout on tips would cannibalize subscriptions
- Apple has App Store restrictions on tipping (takes 30% cut)
- YouTube's tipping is buried; not integrated with music platform

**NGN's advantage**: Sparks are core to product, not afterthought.

## 4. Revenue Stream #3: Ticketing (20-25% of Revenue)

### 4.1 How It Works

Artists can link their NGN profile to ticketing platforms (Ticketmaster, Bandsintown, Eventbrite).

NGN integration provides:
- Live event data (concert dates, venues)
- Fan purchase tracking
- Cross-promotion to NGN audience

**Revenue model**:
- 2.5% of ticket price
- $1.50 per ticket (flat)
- Charged to artist at point of sale

**Example**:
- Artist sells 1,000 tickets at $50 each = $50K revenue
- NGN fee: (2.5% × $50K) + (1,000 × $1.50) = $1,250 + $1,500 = $2,750
- Artist nets: $47,250

### 4.2 Unit Economics: Ticketing

#### Revenue Per Ticket

| Ticket Price | Platform Fee | NGN Revenue | Artist Revenue |
|--------------|-------------|------------|-----------------|
| $20 | $2.00 | $2.00 | $18.00 |
| $50 | $2.50 | $2.50 | $47.50 |
| $100 | $2.50 | $2.50 | $97.50 |

**Key insight**: Flat $1.50 fee creates margin at higher price points.

#### Expected Ticketing Volume

**By 2027** (10,000 artists):
- ~2,000 artists with live tours (20% of platform)
- Average concerts per artist: 12/year = 24K events
- Average attendance: 150 people = 3.6M tickets
- Average ticket price: $45
- **Total ticket revenue**: $162M
- **NGN take (2.5% + $1.50)**: ~$4M

**Processing costs**: ~$500K
**Ticketing gross profit: $3.5M**

### 4.3 Why Ticketing Is Strategic

**Revenue generation**: 3.5M → 4.5M by 2030 = significant % of total revenue

**Network effects**:
- More artists with tours → More fans buy tickets through NGN → More data on who attends concerts
- This data becomes valuable for:
  - Venue booking recommendations ("You attended 5 indie rock shows; here are similar upcoming events")
  - Artist targeting ("These fans bought tickets to similar artists; here's your audience")
  - Regional trends ("Hip-hop is trending in Seattle; venue recommendations")

**Defensibility**:
- Spotify/Apple don't integrate live event data (focused on streaming)
- Ticketmaster/Live Nation need artist data (NGN becomes valuable partner)
- First platform to connect listening → live events wins this moat

### 5.2 Unit Economics: B2B Services

**Total B2B Revenue (2027)**: ~$2.36M
**Processing/support costs**: ~$400K
**B2B gross profit: $1.96M** (83% margin)

**High-margin business**: B2B services have 80%+ margins (no payment processing, low support cost at scale).

### 5.3 Why B2B Is Strategic

**Revenue concentration**: B2B insulates NGN from consumer preference shifts
- If Spark tips decline, B2B grows
- If subscriptions saturate, B2B expands
- Independent revenue drivers

**Defensibility**:
- Spotify/Apple don't offer B2B services (don't have artist data)
- Aggregators (DistroKid) could compete, but NGN's transparent data is better
- First mover advantage in artist infrastructure data licensing

**Enterprise relationships**:
- Labels become NGN partners (not competitors)
- Aggregators integrate with NGN (not replace)
- Creates "ecosystem" rather than "competitor" dynamic

## 7. Unit Economics: Customer Acquisition & Lifetime Value

### 7.1 Artist LTV (Lifetime Value)

**What we pay to acquire an artist**:

| Channel | CAC | Efficiency |
|---------|-----|-----------|
| Organic (word-of-mouth) | $0 | Viral |
| Radio station referrals | $50 | High |
| Aggregator integration | $100 | Medium |
| Paid advertising | $150 | Low |
| **Platform average** | **$75** | — |

**What an artist earns over 3 years**:

| Artist Tier | Avg Annual | 3-Year LTV |
|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Inactive | $0 | $0 |
| Active | $400 (Sparks) + $200 (Subs) | $1,800 |
| Growing | $2,000 + $500 | $7,500 |
| Successful | $10,000+ | $30,000+ |
| **Blended average** | **$1,500** | **$4,500** |

### 7.2 LTV/CAC Ratio (Key Metric)

**LTV/CAC = $4,500 / $75 = 60x**

**Industry benchmark**:
- SaaS healthy ratio: 3-5x
- Exceptional SaaS: 5-10x
- NGN: 60x (because platform takes small cut from artists)

**This is exceptionally high.** Why?

Because NGN isn't selling to artists; NGN is *enabling* artist revenue. When artists earn $4,500 over 3 years, they're generating value for themselves (not NGN). NGN's $75 CAC is tiny relative to artist economic value created.

**Comparison**:
- Spotify CAC: ~$50 (marketing to listeners)
- Spotify LTV: ~$200 (per listener over lifetime)
- Spotify LTV/CAC: 4x

- NGN CAC: ~$75 (referrals + organic)
- NGN LTV: ~$4,500 (per artist over lifetime, including all revenue streams)
- NGN LTV/CAC: 60x ✅

### 7.3 Payback Period

**Payback period** = How long before CAC is recovered

For NGN:
- CAC: $75
- Average annual LTV: $1,500
- **Payback period: 0.3 months (20 days)**

**This is insanely fast.** Most SaaS companies have 12-18 month payback periods.

**Why?** Because Spark tips and subscriptions provide immediate revenue, while most SaaS products take months to generate value.

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

### 9.1 Revenue Risk: What If Artist Adoption Slows?

**Risk**: Artists don't onboard as fast; revenue lags projections

**Mitigation**:
- Subscriptions grow faster than projections (lower friction)
- B2B licensing grows (labels/aggregators adopt earlier)
- Ticketing scales (with fewer artists)

**Expected impact**: Delays break-even by 6-12 months, but business case still works

### 9.2 Unit Economics Risk: What If CAC Increases?

**Risk**: Paid advertising becomes more expensive; organic growth plateaus

**Mitigation**:
- Radio station partnerships lower CAC (currently $50)
- Viral loops (artist invites other artists)
- Aggregator partnerships (reach millions of artists organically)

**Expected impact**: CAC rises to $150-200, but LTV still 20-30x (healthy)

### 9.3 Regulatory Risk: What If Apple/EU Restrict Tips?

**Risk**: Apple charges 30% on in-app tips; EU restricts micropayments

**Mitigation**:
- 60% of revenue from non-tip streams (subscriptions, ticketing, B2B)
- Direct-to-artist payouts not affected
- Regulatory environment already leaning toward micropayment support

**Expected impact**: Spark margin declines 10-20%, but overall business grows
